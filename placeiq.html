PlaceIQ Branding

I joined PlaceIQ as the first full time designer, so needless to say the branding needed work. The CEO was very fond of the logo, so instead of fully rebanding I just updated the logo. My goal was to give it a friendlier, more modern look, and still keep the basic structure in place.

Colors

The previous materials were scattered with every hue and shade of purple and gray, sprinkled with neon green, a color already in use by a competitor. When deciding colors, I originally played around with softer, less vivid purples, but decided that a strong unique purple would be memorable and stand out in the market. It was also apparent that with so many detailed maps, diagrams, and charts, having a nice selection of supporting colors would be necessary.

Style Guide

I worked in collaboration with my team to develop the style guide for PlaceIQ. I wanted the end result to be a comprehensive brand book that would function as a strong reference when building materials, and ultimately keep materials consistant in an ever changing company.

Type

Being a B2B company, the primary marketing material at first, was PowerPoint slides that were often emailed. The only way to preseve the appearance when sending these materials would be to use web safe fonts. However, I still wanted to have the ability to use more refined fonts that would work for future materials. So after deciding on the web fonts, I selected similar style fonts for print.

Photography

Due to small budgets and an ever evolving business, there was a heavy reliance on stock imagery. However, we needed to ensure our materials would look consistant without looking generic. I worked to hand select and refine the photos we were using, and develop the overall photo style.
